Privacy Policy

Confidentiality in psychotherapy is a vital ethical and legal tenet that safeguards clients' privacy and fosters trust between therapists and clients. The following are the major features of confidentiality guidelines and policies in psychotherapy –

As a client, you have the right to:

Privacy of Your Information – Your therapist cannot share what you discuss in therapy without your written permission.
Access to Your Records – You may request a copy of your therapy records, though therapists may provide a summary instead of full session notes.
Informed Consent – Before therapy begins, you should receive a confidentiality agreement explaining how your information will be handled.
Secure Storage of Records – Therapists must store physical and electronic records securely to prevent unauthorized access.
Confidentiality may be broken if:

Risk of Harm to Self or Others - If a client expresses serious intent to harm themselves (suicide) or another person (homicide), the therapist must take action to prevent harm. This may involve contacting emergency services, family members, or other professionals.

Court Orders or Legal Proceedings - A therapist may be required to release records or testify in court if ordered by a judge. Some exceptions may apply, such as therapist-client privilege in certain legal cases.

Payment Policies:

1. Payment of Session Fees

a) Clients are required to make timely payments at the time of service using the online payment methods provided by the counselor.

b) Payment must be made prior to each session on a per-session basis. If multiple sessions are booked, payment can be made in advance for all sessions or before each individual session.

c) Failure to make timely payments may result in the suspension of counselling sessions at the counsellor’s discretion.

2. Cancellations, Rescheduling & Late Arrivals

a) Clients are expected to be on time for scheduled appointments.
b) If you anticipate being late, please notify the psychologist at least two (2) hours in advance.
c) Cancellations or rescheduling requests must be made at least twenty-four (24) hours in advance to avoid charges. Missed appointments and last-minute cancellations will be charged the full session fee.
d) If a client arrives late, the session will still end at the scheduled time without an extension.
e) In rare cases where the therapist needs to cancel or reschedule within 24 hours of the session, the client will receive the next session at no cost as compensation.
